Voice and Tone

The voice and tone that Unum uses should reflect our three cornerstones: simplicity, empathy, and expertise.The ways we communicate and the language we use defines our brand and differentiates us in the marketplace.

Unum means one

The origins of our company name come from our country’s proud motto, E. pluribus unum; “out of many, one.” When communicating on behalf of our brand, it’s important to remember we have a similar responsibility to present with one consistent and unified voice.  Follow the guidance in this section when developing communications to ensure consistency of tone and voice across all internal and external audiences.

Simplicity

Making it simple makes all the difference.

Our brand cornerstones build upon each other to help deliver the desired customer experience. In this way, expertise and empathy guide us to create simpler, more customer-focused solutions and communications.

Say what you need to say with as few words possible. Never compromise understanding for efficiency.

How to communicate simplicity

  • Write conversationally and use short sentences whenever possible
  • Consider not only what you need to say but the audiences’ capacity to receive
  • Choose the right channel; don’t send an email when a conversation is required
  • Sending a communication doesn’t mean it will be read; invite the audience in
  • Don’t explain, instead communicate with patience to ensure understanding
  • See the Additional Writing Tips section of this for more guidance on style
 

Empathy

Our perspective is built on empathy and heart.

Unum is a company operating on a set of principles that are fundamental to the human need to feel safe; to feel cared for; to belong. We demonstrate our commitment to these guiding principles through dedication to community service, with the billions in claims paid each year, and in the actions of our people every day.

Empathy is a matter of what we say and how we say it. Every communication is a chance to show we care. Our business is serious, but it doesn’t have to be cold and impersonal.

How to write with empathy

  • Put yourself in the customer’s position and prioritize what’s important to them
  • In any situation, use a tone that you’d expect as if you were the recipient 
  • Acknowledge audience pain points and frustrations, and offer solutions 
  • Consider every communication an opportunity to further the relationship 
  • Don’t over-communicate in frequency or volume; be respectful of time and needs
  • When in doubt, think of someone you care for and imagine they’re the audience
 

Expertise

Expertise is always thinking ahead.

Being an expert takes more than rote knowledge and book smarts. It’s an unending quest to answer why, coupled with the unique ability to turn a natural inquisitiveness into practical know-how.

Expertise speaks with self-assured confidence. Succinctly and factually, without hyperbole or undue editorializing. People rely on us to be experts in everything we do. Be careful not to confuse your audience with complexity.

How to convey expertise

  • Stick to the facts in context; don’t educate or explain too much
  • Properly cite all sources, internal and external, as necessary
  • Avoid acronyms, industry-specific terms and complex jargon
  • Use familiar/relative concepts to demonstrate practical solutions
  • Do not use worst-case scenarios or “threat marketing” to make a point
  • Follow all AP Guidelines for consistency in punctuation and style
  • Avoid using hyperbole and exclamation points to ensure an expert voice
