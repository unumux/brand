Videography Guidelines

Videography and Motion Design

As with printed materials, our brand is maintained in video and motion design using a set of related guidelines and graphic standards. While our brand advertising follows specific campaign guidelines, video and animation work including subject matter expert interviews, customer testimonial, product overviews, social media and promotional videos also share a common look, feel and style that make our brand stand out in the marketplace. Follow the information in this section to ensure your video and motion projects are consistent and aligned with the Unum brand.

Style Preferences

Our brand advertising introduces and aesthetic that's defined by its use of natural light and authentic, slice of life moments that combine to create a genuine (not highly produced) look and feel. Whenever possible additional video work should similarly leverage elements that contribute to this aesthetic.

These include:

  • Using organic/natural lighting, including shooting into the source
  • Setting a shallow depth of field to bring the action/focal point to the fore
  • Incorporating over exposed backgrounds, light leaks, etc.

Video Aquisition

Live action is the preferred format for all Unum videos. Be sure to incorporate the following technical specifications to ensure consistency when capturing new live video action video.

  • 1080p or higher for resolution
  • 24p (23.98) Frames Per Second for all real-time footage
  • Over-cranked video should be 60p or higher if necessary
  • Shutter speed should be 2X frame rate (faster only if necessary)
  • Try to use the native ISO/ASA of camera
  • Link out to tech specs on Box?

Use of Stock Video

It is often the case that our videos require the use of stock assets for b-roll or other supplemental footage. Use the guidelines included in this section around style preferences to ensure your stock video selections support the overall Unum brand. Be sure lighting and backgrounds for stock selections fit with the look and feel of your acquired video to avoid jarring scene changes or incompatible styles. And be sure to pay close attention to usage rights and licensing to ensure proper payment and protect the company from liability.

Prepping Video for Social Media

Leveraging video through social media channels (e.g. Facebook) has proven to be an effective way to share our stories. In general, video distributed through social media must be modified prior to publishing to support user expectations and the norms of the medium, as well as technical specifications. Keep in mind that most often videos displayed through social media do not include sound, so at minumum supers should be used to convey necessary messaging.

Follow the guidance here for applying supers to videos for social media:

  • Static type
  • Kinetic type

In addition to following all brand standards for style, color and type, the following guidelines should be considered when preparing videos for social media:

  • Keep ratio at 16.9
  • Max size is 1280 x 720 (or 2mb)
  • Center important information (any info not centered will be cut off)
  • Keep text under 20% of the whole image; text is important, but keep the amount of information extremely limited and readable
  • When making playlists, keep videos of specific content grouped together by similar thumbnails so users can assume what they're about to consume
  • Use high-contrast so visual information and text is legible when viewed at thumbnail and mobile size
